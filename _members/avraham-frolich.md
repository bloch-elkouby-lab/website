Avraham graduated from Yeshiva University with a B.A. in English, with minors in Psychology and Music. In recent years, he has worked in clinical environments and in humanitarian roles with the American Red Cross. His academic interests include suicide assessment and prevention, as well as the ways memory, identity, and narrative shape individuals across generations. In addition to his research interests, Avraham writes and records music, watches baseball, and writes fiction and essays when he can find the time.
